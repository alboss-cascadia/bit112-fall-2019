Web Authoring - BIT 112

Students learn the basics of designing and creating web sites including HTML and CSS, image manipulation, page layout, file transfer, and internet protocols. Students consider website design principles, create several sites, and test them on the web. Special emphasis is placed on using professional software, applying industry standards, and managing multiple projects.

Learning outcomes

  • Learn Actively Effectively: Use HTML and CSS standards to create web pages. Work with key website development tools including Photoshop, Dreamweaver, and Git.
  • Think Critically, Creatively and Reflectively: Structure a multi-page website to easily handle changes and growth
  • Communicate with Clarity and Originality: Develop and present website designs that communicate effectively to appropriate audience.
  • Interact in Diverse and Complex Environments: Engage in work, study, and conversation on the topics of web authoring with colleagues

Assessment

Your proficiency in the learning outcomes is assessed using individual exercises, assignments, and reports, along with team problem-solving in discussion forums. Course activities may involve individual work and group work. Individual assessments will often be used in combination with class activities.

Discussion forums will describe your understanding and contain your questions from viewing the online materials that are part of the required material for the course.

Quizzes will assess students’ content mastery from the readings, viewing and coursework. Quizzes are individual, open-book assessments. No make-up quizzes allowed.

Portfolio Exercises will display each student’s course work for review and grading in a course portfolio. The portfolios will be assessed as a student developed website in their own right.

Lab Assignments will include a variety of individual and peer activities, both in-class and beyond the class time. This work will allow you to practice the current course knowledge and skills. You will generally post the results of your work on labs to your course portfolio.

Research Reports work should answer the particular research questions completely in your own words, citing well-documented sources. Project reports will be posted to your course portfolio.

Personal Website Project will be a focus during the second half of the class. Each student will propose, plan, design, create, and test a sample website. The four main phases of the project culminate in a complete website reviewed by the class with an optional class presentation.
